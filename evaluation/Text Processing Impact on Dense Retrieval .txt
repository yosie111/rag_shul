Text Processing Impact on Dense Retrieval (600 queries, Shulchan Aruch)

Stage 1 — Vowelized (raw Torat Emet text with nikud)
Recall@1 = 9% · Recall@50 = 60% · 408 hits
Nikud characters severely hurt embedding models. Rank distribution is flat and spread across all positions.

Stage 2 — Unvowelized, defective spelling (nikud stripped only)
Recall@1 = 47% · Recall@50 = 91% · 558 hits · MRR ≈ 0.55
Dramatic jump from simply removing nikud. Most hits concentrate at ranks 0–3.

Stage 3 — Fully processed (ktiv male + semantic unification)
Recall@1 = 52% · Recall@50 = 92% · MRR = 0.62
Additional gains from full spelling conversion, name unification (divine names → אלוהים, goy variants → גוי), abbreviation expansion, gematria-to-words, and reference deletion.

Stage 4 — Breadcrumb prefix (hierarchical context)
Recall@1 = 60% · Recall@50 = 95% · MRR = 0.70
Each seif is prefixed with [אורח חיים > הלכות group > siman title], giving the embedding model topical context. Recall@15 crosses the 90% target for the first time (90.8%).

Bottom line:
The full pipeline improved Recall@1 from 9% → 60% (6.7×) and MRR from ~0.1 → 0.70 (7×).
At K=1 alone — the single top result being correct — each stage contributed a measurable lift:
9% → 47% → 52% → 60%.
The 90% recall target is reached at K=15 (vs. not reached even at K=50 with raw text).
